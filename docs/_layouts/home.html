Why Symbolia?

A technological breakthrough is only truly a breakthrough if it unifies previously disparate areas of one's life. Personal computing has unified mail, shopping, television, etc. into the more general idea of information transfer. As the technology behind virtual reality improves, it becomes clearer that VR is here to stay. But how do we show the power of VR?

Symbolia aims to explore interactions with math that are possible in VR, that could not have been previously achieved. Nowadays, when working through derivations, mathematicians find themselves flitting between creative mediums (e.g., whiteboards and notebooks) and computers---the former being used for exploratory work and the latter being used for verifying the exploratory work. More concretely, suppose you're working through a problem with a term $$log(x + y)$$, and you mistakenly rewrite this into $$log(x) + log(y)$$. You carry on and later on in your derivation, you find you can't show your desired conclusion, or even worse, you derive a conclusion that you don't realize is false! Even expert mathematicians make silly mistakes, but we often don't check for these kinds of mistakes. Symbolia integrates computational tools into a virtual whiteboard environment, to make checking your work part of the creative process. Beyond these concrete benefits, Symbolia aims to "feel" fun and natural and to allow its users to more consistently hit flow states when playing with math.
